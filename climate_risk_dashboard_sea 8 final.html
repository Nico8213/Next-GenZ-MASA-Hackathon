Key Insights
  • 01 GHG emissions are rising in both countries, driven by industrial growth and energy demand — a systemic risk for the SEA reinsurance portfolio.
  • 02 Philippines faces higher disaster frequency from typhoon belt exposure, with episodic large-loss events (Haiyan 2013: $920M insured).
  • 03 Malaysia shows a rising flood-claim trend driven by deforestation and floodplain urbanisation — 2021 Selangor megaflood signals structural change.
  • 04 Positive GHG–claims correlation (r > 0.7) confirms climate as a material systemic risk driver for reinsurance pricing models.
  • 05 NDC-aligned 2%/yr mitigation saves ~87k kt CO₂eq combined by 2030. Use the custom rate slider to explore scenarios beyond preset values.
Limitations & Uncertainties
  • ! OLS/Poly models assume trend continuation — structural breaks (COVID-19, ENSO, policy shifts) are not explicitly modelled.
  • ! Low insurance penetration in SEA (MY ~4%, PH ~1.7% GDP) means claims data significantly understate true economic losses.
  • ! Custom mitigation rate is illustrative — real-world effectiveness depends on enforcement and political compliance.
  • ! No temperature, sea-level rise, or SST co-variates. Short post-2000 series limits statistical precision.

Model Evaluation & Selection

To ensure robustness, we evaluated multiple regression models. While Random Forest showed slight overfitting, Polynomial/Linear Regression was selected for the final dashboard due to its superior interpretability for actuarial scenario testing.
